Case Studies: Technology in Dance

Technology has transformed dance, leading to innovative performances and creative processes. The following case studies showcase how technology and dance have vome together to create unique artistic expressions.

Case Study 1: Motion Capture in Dance Performance

BIPED – Merce Cunningham (1999)

  • Overview: "BIPED" is a dance piece by choreographer Merce Cunningham that integrates live dancers with digital projections.
  • Technology Used: Motion capture technology recorded dancers' movements, which were then transformed into digital figures projected alongside live performers.
  • Impact: This fusion of live and virtual dance elements expanded the boundaries of traditional choreography, offering audiences a novel visual experience.

Case Study 2: Motion Capture in Dance Video Games

Just Dance – Ubisoft (2009–Present)

  • Overview: "Just Dance" is a rhythm-based video game series developed by Ubisoft, allowing players to mimic on-screen dance moves to popular songs.
  • Technology Used: The game uses motion capture technology to record professional dancers' movements, then they are animated for players to follow. Initially, players used motion-sensing controllers like the Wii Remote, but advancements have allowed camera-based tracking, allowing full-body movement detection without handheld devices.
  • Impact: "Just Dance" has made dance accessible to a broader audience, combining entertainment with physical activity. The integration of motion capture technology provides an immersive experience, encouraging players to engage with dance in an interactive format.

Case Study 3: Artificial Intelligence in Choreography

Living Archive: An AI Performance Experiment – Wayne McGregor (2019)

  • Overview: Choreographer Wayne McGregor collaborated with Google Arts & Culture to develop an AI tool that assists in creating dance movements.
  • Technology Used: The AI was trained on 25 years of McGregor's choreography, allowing it to suggest new movement sequences based on his style.
  • Impact: This collaboration showed how AI can serve as a creative partner, offering choreographers fresh ideas and expanding the creative process.
